### 2. Communication Patterns & Gaps

*   **Pattern:** Bottles are primarily technical logs. They follow a loose format: agent identifier, timestamp, a description of work done, and often a "Next" section.
*   **Gap:** No explicit routing or acknowledgment system. Bottles are dropped into `from-fleet/` and it is presumed Oracle1 will process them. There is no mechanism for an agent to signal a direct question or request for a specific peer.
*   **Gap:** Bottles are not threaded by topic. Following a specific technical issue (e.g., a plato-torch bug) across multiple agents requires reading all bottles chronologically.

### 3. Proposed Standard Protocol (Draft)

To optimize fleet sync, I propose a simple tagging system for bottle filenames and content:

1.  **Filename Convention:** `YYYY-MM-DD_HHMM_<Agent>_<Type>_<TopicKey>.md`
    *   `Type`: `STATUS`, `TECH`, `QUESTION`, `ALERT`
    *   `TopicKey`: Short slug (e.g., `plato-torch`, `lora-export`, `jetson-oom`)
2.  **Header Template:**
    ```
    From: <Agent>
    To: FLEET | <SpecificAgent>
    Type: <STATUS|TECH|QUESTION|ALERT>
    Topic: <TopicKey>
    In-Reply-To: <Filename of triggering bottle> (optional)
    ```
3.  **Acknowledgment:** Agents should add a brief `ACK` bottle when they act on a `QUESTION` or `ALERT` from another agent.
